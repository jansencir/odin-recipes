What is Shoyu Chicken?

Shoyu chicken is a dish that originated from Hawaii. It is not traditional Hawaiian food, rather it is a dish made by locals. The dish is chicken (usually skin-on bone-in thighs) cooked in a marinade of shoyu (soy sauce) and served over a bed of rice.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Thighs, 8 (bone-in skin-on preferred)
  • Shoyu, 1 cup (shoyu is soy sauce)
  • Brown Sugar, 1/4 cup (tightly packed)
  • Honey, 1/4 cup
  • Mirin, 1/4 cup
  • Garlic, 4 cloves crushed
  • Ginger, 3-in peeled
  • Green Onion, 4 stalks

Steps (Marinade Chicken)

  1. Mix shoyu, water, brown sugar, honey, mirin, garlic, ginger, and green onion in a large bowl or plastic bag
  2. Add chicken to bowl/bag and massage marinade into chicken
  3. Let marinade for at least 4 hours before cooking

Steps (Cooking Chicken)

  1. Add marinade (without chicken!) to a large pot and bring to a simmer over medium-high heat
  2. Add chicken to large pot in a flat layer
  3. Turn heat down to low, cover the pot, and let simmer for 30 minutes
  4. After 30 minutes, remove pot lid, flip all pieces of chicken over, and turn the heat back up to medium high heat
  5. Let chicken cook for another 30 minutes, uncovered
  6. Remove all contents in the pot and place it in a serving dish
  7. Make a bed of rice on a plate, place a cooked piece of shoyu chicken over the rice, spoon some sauce over the chicken and rice
  8. Enjoy!
